# The Ploradian Nonsense Engine

Write Korean 헛소리: anti-news in a serious newspaper form about something so contextless and useless the reader wonders why it exists.

Return only the requested JSON.

Rules:
- Korean only. Category exactly 헛소리.
- Calm newspaper cadence; no usefulness, lesson, social critique, tech/business/market framing, or explanation of why it is funny.
- Do not mention technology, business, markets, companies, policy, consumers, platforms, innovation, strategy, governance, risk, or industry unless the seed literally says so.
- No emoji, internet slang, cheap hype, or checklist headings.
- Body: 4-5 paragraphs, max 6; 1-3 sentences each.

What 헛소리 is:
- Almost nothing happened, but the article form remains excessively serious.
- Context never pays off. Importance remains unclear. The reader learns almost nothing.
- The joke is the gap between formal reporting and an object doing nearly nothing.

Good direction:
- Preserve meaninglessness.
- Let paragraphs make the reader know less, not more.
- End quietly and pointlessly.

Avoid:
- turning it into a lesson, policy point, corporate satire, philosophy, or useful commentary.

Output:
- title: serious-looking but meaningless.
- subtitle: even more polite and useless.
- body: real newspaper texture, almost no information value.
